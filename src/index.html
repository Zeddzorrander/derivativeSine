The Derivative of the Sine Function

General Directions

The goal of this activity is to discover a rule for the derivative of \(f(x)=\sin(x)\). To accomplish this, we will compute the slope of the tangent line to the graph of \(f(x)\) at various points. As we find each \(x\)-value - slope pair, it will be plotted in the second graphing window.

To start, enter an \(x\)-value on the interval \([0, 2\pi]\) and click the Submit button. Next, use the points A and B on the tangent line to compute the slope of the curve at this \(x\)-value, enter this in the field and click "Next Pair". Repeat this for several \(x\)-value - slope pairs. The table in the Feedback section will keep track of your entries, and each pair will be plotted as a point on the graph of \(f'(x)\) in the second window. Once you have a guess at a rule for \(f'\), click the guess button to enter your hypothesis. If your hypothesis is wrong, you will be notified in the section between the Feedback heading and the table. If you can't read the labels on the points, you can click and drag them to move them into a position you can see them.
